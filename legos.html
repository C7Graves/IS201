What Is LEGO?

LEGO is a line of plastic construction toys manufactured by the LEGO Group, a privately held company based in Billund, Denmark. The company's flagship product consists of colorful interlocking plastic bricks that can be assembled in countless ways — then disassembled and built again.

Founded in 1932 by Ole Kirk Christiansen, LEGO has grown into the world's largest toy company by revenue, selling over 400 billion bricks to date.

Why LEGO?

Building with LEGO isn't just play — it's problem solving, spatial reasoning, and creativity all in one brick-shaped package. Here's what makes it special:

  1. Creativity & Open-Ended Play
    • No single "right" way to build
    • Encourages imagination and original thinking
    • Millions of possible combinations from a standard brick set
  2. STEM Education
    • Teaches structural engineering principles
    • LEGO Mindstorms introduces robotics and coding
    • Used in classrooms worldwide from K-12 to university
  3. Multigenerational Appeal
    • Sets range from Duplo for toddlers to 10,000+ piece expert builds
    • Adult fans of LEGO (AFOLs) are a massive community
    • Sets hold and gain value as collectibles
  4. Sustainability Commitment
    • Goal to use sustainable materials in all core products by 2030
    • Already producing plant-based elements from sugarcane
